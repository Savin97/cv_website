Breakwater

Earnings-period risk engine for identifying stocks and setups with elevated probability of large post-earnings moves.

What I built

Breakwater combines event-aware feature engineering, historical earnings behavior, volatility expansion, and momentum stress into a scoring framework that ranks earnings events by tail-risk intensity.

Problem Earnings events create clustered, high-impact moves. Most generic indicators do a poor job isolating when the risk is truly elevated.
Approach Focus on concentration of tail events: volatility stretch, earnings explosiveness, and momentum fragility combined into a rankable risk framework.
Output Dashboard views, stock-level risk flags, interpretable score components, and report-ready summaries for decision support.

Technical breakdown

Data S&P 500 price history, earnings dates and surprise-related fields, sector information, rolling return and volatility statistics.
Features Volatility expansion, pre-event drift, momentum stretch, stock-vs-sector stress, historical reaction distributions, event-window context.
Architecture stage1 ingestion → stage2 feature engineering → stage3 scoring → stage4 outputs → stage5 reporting/backtesting.
Stack Python, pandas, numpy, Streamlit, Git, modular pipeline design.
